• Description of Product Use:
• In the interest of conserving their precious resources of detergent supplies, hot water, occupied washer and dryer space, and time allotted to doing chores, Seth and his roommates at the beginning of the semester implemented their own “house policy”: of sorting their collective dirty clothing and linens into each of the traditional five main groups – Lights, Darks, Brights, Whites and Delicates (please refer to citation [3.] under the “Works Cited” section of this document) – and then waiting until any one of these sorted piles has accumulated a sufficiently large quantity or volume of clothing to proceed with running a (“heavy”) wash cycle.
•
• As the most “app-savvy” iPhone owner in the household, Seth has fallen into the role of overseeing the roundup, sorting and loading of his and his roommates’ laundry while the others are attending to other housework assignments. A few times per week, he uses the “Laundri-Map” app on his phone to “Create New”,”Open Saved”, and/or “Import [Merge]” Laundry Lists for organizing his and his roommates’ laundry and sorting it for washer or dryer loads, grouped according to the aforementioned sorting categories and machine cycle settings. After Seth has programmed the day’s dirty clothing/linen items into his phone – identifying the color, fabric content and product type for each, with just a few a clicks  – he then adds this data to his ongoing Closet Galleries folder for laundry inventory that is integrated into the app, where he can pull any of those items up instantly and place them in a new list, the next time they need to be washed.
