USER SCENARIO
•
•The following fictional narrative is envisioned to explain a hypothetical scenario whereby the “average, typical” user discovered, located and used the product:
• 
• Mikaela, a first-year college student, was surfing the apps available from the App Store portal on her iPhone, with the immediate goal of finding one of the latest “outfit creator” fashion assistance apps that she had heard about from some of her friends in the freshman girls’ dorm. Among the first keywords she thought to enter in her quest for such an app was “clothing” – consequentially, the newly released “Laundri-Map Pocket Laundry Assistant” app appeared within the first pool of relevant featured app products, and although it wasn’t what she had had in mind, the name and thumbnail logo graphic caught her eye as she scrolled through the top hits. Clicking to expand the product description for this not-so-common sounding mobile wizard, Mikaela was intrigued by the features that the “Laundri-Map” claimed to offer; too bad that it wasn’t among the “FREE” downloads interspersing the other pay-to-install apps in the featured list. Still, she thought: for what seemed to offer a highly extensive package of interactive functions, combined within such a a conveniently handy and user-friendly app – the pricetag of a few bucks might not actually pose a problem, in this case…
•
• Within about a week of purchasing and installing the “Laundri-Map” app to her iPhone, Mikaela made a full night of it at the laundry room in the lobby of her residence hall. For the first time all school year, she found herself going through a whole roll of quarters, with the aid of the laundry sorting and machine setting instructions which the app’s “Create/Add To Loads” features calculated for her; in order to get all of her dirty laundry – some of it months in the making – thoroughly sorted, properly cleaned, fully dried, and not even shrunk or discolored at any point!
•
• Making pleasant conversation with some of the other girls who joined her in emptying or filling their own laundry baskets that evening, Mikaela spread the word about the new laundry aid that she had discovered for her iPhone. She even aided one of the other girls in selecting the right wash and rinse settings for cleaning a delicate cardigan sweater. Within weeks, the “Laundri-Map Pocket Laundry Assistant” had found a user base among a growing number of first-year college students in Mikaela’s dorm.
